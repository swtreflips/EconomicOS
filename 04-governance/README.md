# Phase 4: Open-Source Governance

> **Status: Future** -- Governance design should evolve alongside Phases 1-3 but becomes critical at scale.

## Purpose

econOS is open-source infrastructure. Governance isn't about controlling the system -- it's about maintaining its quality, credibility, and openness as it grows.

In the Venezuelan context, this has a specific dimension: econOS provides economic data independent of any government. It must be designed so that no government, institution, or interest group can co-opt or suppress it. The BCV demonstrated what happens when economic data is politically controlled. econOS is the antidote.

## What Governance Means Here

- **Methodology transparency**: How are indicators calculated? What data sources are used? What are the known biases? All of this is public.
- **Open-source code**: Anyone can audit, fork, or run their own instance of econOS.
- **Community contribution**: Economists, data scientists, developers from Venezuela, Colombia, and the diaspora contribute to improving models, adding data sources, and validating outputs.
- **Quality control**: Peer review of methodology. Version control of model changes. Clear documentation of data source additions and removals.
- **Resistance to capture**: No single funder, institution, or government controls the project. Distributed maintenance, like Linux or Wikipedia.

## Core Tension

The system must be:
- **Technically rigorous** enough to be credible (requires expert input)
- **Accessible** enough to be useful (requires plain-language communication)
- **Resilient** enough to survive political pressure (requires distributed control)
- **Adaptable** enough to evolve (requires clear contribution processes)

## The LatAm Governance Challenge

In Venezuela specifically:
- The government may view independent economic data as politically threatening
- Data sources may be pressured or restricted
- Contributors may face political risk

The open-source, distributed model mitigates this: there's no single server to shut down, no single organization to pressure, no single person to silence. The project can be maintained from Caracas, from Bogota, from Miami, from Madrid -- wherever the diaspora is.

## Depends On

- Operational experience from Phases 1-3 (governance designed in advance of the thing it governs is almost always wrong)
- A community of contributors (which grows organically from Phase 1 usage)
